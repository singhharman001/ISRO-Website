About Satellite Communication

Satellite communication plays a vital role in modern technology by enabling global connectivity and data transmission. It utilizes artificial satellites to relay signals across vast distances, bridging remote areas with urban hubs. This technology is instrumental in broadcasting television, enabling internet services, supporting GPS systems, and facilitating disaster management. By orbiting the Earth, communication satellites provide uninterrupted signals over large geographic areas, overcoming obstacles like terrain and distance. With advancements in satellite technology, such as high-throughput satellites and constellations, satellite communication continues to revolutionize industries and enhance global communication infrastructure.

Purpose: ISRO's satellite communication initiatives aim to provide reliable communication services for various applications, including broadcasting, telemedicine, distance education, disaster management, and national security.

How It Works

Satellites are marvels of engineering that play a critical role in modern communication, navigation, Earth observation, and scientific research. A satellite functions by orbiting the Earth or another celestial body, using its onboard systems to collect, process, and transmit data. Satellites operate through a combination of advanced communication equipment, sensors, and power systems. Positioned in space after being launched via rockets, their orbits—whether geostationary, polar, or elliptical—are determined based on their purpose.

A satellite's primary communication role involves transponders, which receive signals from Earth, amplify them, and retransmit them to a target location. This technology is foundational for global broadcasting, internet services, and long-distance telecommunications. Weather satellites monitor atmospheric conditions, while navigation satellites like ISRO's NAVIC provide precise positioning data. Earth observation satellites capture high-resolution images for applications in agriculture, disaster management, and urban planning.

ISRO’s satellites, such as the GSAT series for communication and the RISAT series for Earth observation, exemplify the diverse capabilities of satellite technology. The INSAT series has revolutionized meteorology and broadcasting in India, while missions like the Mars Orbiter Mission demonstrate ISRO’s interplanetary research achievements.

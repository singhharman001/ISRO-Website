About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India, headquartered in Bengaluru, India. Founded in 1969, ISRO's mission is to harness space technology for national development while pursuing space science research and planetary exploration.

ISRO has been at the forefront of space exploration, with its successful missions like Chandrayaan, Mangalyaan, and the Gaganyaan human spaceflight program. The agency has developed numerous satellites for communications, weather forecasting, and earth observation, playing a pivotal role in advancing India's space capabilities.

With a vision to make India a global leader in space technology, ISRO continues to push boundaries through innovation and collaboration with international space agencies. The agency's work is driven by a commitment to contribute to the socio-economic development of the country through space technology.

ISRO's achievements inspire future generations and represent India’s commitment to space exploration and scientific advancement.

New Satellite Launch Successful

Chandrayaan-3 is India's third lunar exploration mission, developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Launched in 2023, this mission marked a significant milestone in India’s space exploration journey, aimed at demonstrating soft-landing capabilities on the Moon. Unlike its predecessor Chandrayaan-2, Chandrayaan-3 focused solely on the lander and rover components, excluding an orbiter. The lander, named Vikram, successfully landed near the lunar south pole, a region of scientific interest due to its potential water ice deposits. The rover, Pragyan, conducted on-site analysis, transmitting valuable data about the Moon's surface composition. Chandrayaan-3's success reaffirmed India’s prowess in space technology, propelling it into the elite group of nations capable of lunar landings and inspiring advancements in future interplanetary missions..

Upcoming Mars Mission

ISRO’s upcoming Mars missions aim to build upon the success of its groundbreaking Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also known as Mangalyaan. The new initiatives are expected to delve deeper into the mysteries of the Red Planet, focusing on advanced scientific objectives like studying its atmosphere, surface processes, and potential signs of past life. These missions will likely involve cutting-edge technology for precision landing and advanced payloads for more detailed analysis of Martian geology and climate. With these efforts, ISRO seeks to strengthen India's position in interplanetary exploration, contribute to global Mars research, and pave the way for potential human exploration in the future.
